[ The Lazy Jellyfish ]

Scientific Name:

Aurelia Phototropis

Discovered by / at:

Roger, March 2026 / The deep ocean in the p5.js

Family:

The Deep-Sea Sleepers. A group of jellyfish that hate moving and love saving energy.

Habitat:

The Silent Deep Ocean Zone. The jellyfish thrives in no-light environments where currents are strong

Appearance:

It has 150 thin lines that look like soft hair. Most of the time, the colors are dark blue and grey because it is in Power-Saving Mode. Only when it gets excited (or annoyed), it turns bright red for a few seconds.

Key Characteristics:

  • Only moves if it really has to
  • Slowly sink to the button if no stimulation
  • Body pulses very slowly
  • If the water moves, it slowly turns its head to stay in place.

What we know so far about the jellyfish: The Lazy Jellyfish is a deep-sea animal that lives in very cold water. Most jellyfish swim to find food, but this species stays still to save power. Researchers found that it has a very slow heartbeat and a very low metabolism. It spends almost all of its time resting on the ocean floor. In the past, people thought this jellyfish was not alive because it moved so little. But new studies show it is a living creature with a simple brain. It can feel when a human-controlled mouse is nearby. When a mouse moves near it, the jellyfish will follow the cursor slowly. If it is clicked five times, it will enter a "high-energy mode" and move faster for a short time. This creature is very stubborn about its position. When there is a strong water current, the jellyfish will use its energy to turn its body and face the flow. It does this so it doesn't get pushed away. After the current stops or the interaction ends, the jellyfish stops moving and sinks back to the bottom to rest again.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Please be gentle. If you move your mouse near it, it will slowly look at you and follow, but it will get tired quickly. If you want to see it really move, you have to click it many times (about 5 times) to "wake it up." Also, you can change the ocean current with the arrow keys. You will see that the jellyfish is very stubborn—it will turn its body to fight the current just so it doesn't get blown away from its favorite sleeping spot.
